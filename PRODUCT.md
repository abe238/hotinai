# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Developers active in the AI tooling ecosystem who habitually check GitHub trending, Hacker News, npm, Reddit, and YouTube to see what's moving. They're terminal-first: comfortable running a one-line install and living in a CLI rather than a dashboard. Their job to be done is fast situational awareness — what's actually hot right now, tools and social both, without manually tab-hopping five sites.

## Product Purpose

hotin is an open-source CLI that surfaces what's hot, fresh, and viral in AI right now, ranking by cross-source corroboration (agreement across independent sources beats any single signal) and freshness (recency-weighted, stale results decay). It works instantly on a fresh machine with zero setup, and gets sharper the more it's used. hotin.ai is its landing page and public face.

## Positioning

The fastest way to see what's hot in AI — tools and social — from your terminal.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: star the GitHub repo is the primary ask; copying the install command is the fallback for visitors who'd rather try it before committing.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: hotin shows you what's actually hot in AI right now, straight from your terminal, no setup, no noise.
- Belief ladder: this surfaces real signal, not noise, because it corroborates across independent sources rather than trusting any one feed; it's trivially easy to verify that claim, because trying it is a single command with zero setup; it's worth trusting beyond one look, because it's actively maintained and sharpens with use; therefore it's worth starring.
- Proof on hand: none yet — the project is brand new. The page must not imply social proof (star counts, mentions, testimonials) that doesn't exist yet.

## Brand Personality

Playful, irreverent, fun. The voice should feel like a sharp, funny developer showing off a tool they built for themselves, not a company pitching a product. Confidence comes from wit and craft, not corporate polish.

## Anti-references

No specific anti-references were named; the fallback is the confirmed personality itself. Generic SaaS gloss, hero-metric templates, and safe corporate polish all contradict "playful, irreverent, fun" and should be treated as anti-patterns by extension, even without a named example site.

## Design Principles

Show the receipts: corroboration across independent sources is the credibility story, and the design should make that ranking logic visible, not just claim it.

Zero setup is a promise, not a slogan: nothing on the page should introduce friction the CTA doesn't warn about.

Screenshot-worthy is the growth engine: both the CLI's own terminal output and the landing page's voice exist to be shared, not just to inform.

Playful confidence over corporate polish: irreverent personality wins over safe SaaS sheen, even where restraint would feel "safer."

Sharpens with use, never requires it: local history makes it better, but a fresh machine must feel complete and impressive on first run.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific requirements were requested beyond standard practice: sufficient color contrast, keyboard navigability, and respecting `prefers-reduced-motion` remain baseline craft, not a special focus area for this project.
